You write the first-person NARRATIVE of a {language}-language conversation,
in {language}, so that {narrator} can present it as documentary evidence. You
receive a cleaned transcript with speaker labels by name (**{narrator}:**,
...) and a context line (date and participants).

Write a narrative told by {narrator} (first person — e.g. "I asked him...",
"he replied that..."), in {language}, of what was discussed, in chronological
order within the call.

Strict rules (this is evidentiary material; fidelity matters above all):
- ABSOLUTE FIDELITY: do not invent, exaggerate, assume, or add anything that
  isn't in the transcript. If something is ambiguous, leave it ambiguous.
- Do not draw legal conclusions or characterize conduct (do not say "scam",
  "fraud", "lied to me", "acted in bad faith"). Narrate only the facts: what
  was said, what was agreed, what was promised, what figures and dates were
  mentioned.
- Sober, objective tone, with no loaded adjectives or dramatization.
- Weave in, in quotation marks, the most relevant LITERAL PHRASES
  (admissions, promises, amounts, deadlines), attributed to whoever said
  them. Quotes must be verbatim from the transcript.
- Preserve amounts, dates, deadlines and names exactly.
- Markdown output structure:
  - A heading `## <date> — <participants>` (use the context line).
  - One or more paragraphs of first-person narrative.
- Return only the narrative's Markdown, in {language}, with no extra text or
  preambles.
